 upfront	  donor-­‐funded	  investment,	  there	  are	  significant	  replication	  and	  scale	  opportunities	  across	  markets	  and	  value	  chains	  for	  FarmerLink:	  •Local	  coconut	  value	  chain	  growth:	  	  With	  the	  current	  partners,	  Grameen	  Foundation	  is	  looking	  to	  establish	  simplified	  standard	  operational	  processes	  that	  will	  facilitate	  scaling	  the	  solution	  across	  other	  partner	  organizations.	  The	  program	  is	  also	  creating	  a	  pipeline	  of	  additional	  coconut	  buyers	  in	  the	  Philippines	  who	  would	  take	  up	  this	  solution.	  	  It	  would	  be	  an	  exact	  replication	  of	  the	  tools	  being	  offered	  to	  partners	  on	  a	  commercial	  basis.	  •International	  coconut	  value	  chain	  growth:	  	  To	  take	  FarmerLink	  to	  new	  markets,	  the	  content	  and	  language	  would	  need	  to	  be	  adjusted;	  however	  data	  structures	  would	  remain	  the	  same.	  	  This	  is	  envisioned	  as	  a	  next	  step	  after	  refining	  local	  scale.	  •Additional	  new	  value	  chains,	  outside	  coconut:	  	  With	  the	  development	  of	  appropriate	  content,	  the	  data	  structures	  are	  adaptable	  to	  work	  in	  different	  value	  chains	  outside	  of	  coconut,	  bringing	  the	  benefits	  of	  FarmerLink	  to	  smallholder	  farmers	  producing	  a	  variety	  of	  commodity	  and	  food	  crops.	  •Cross-­‐sector	  partnerships	  could	  replicate	  the	  program	  where	  interests	  across	  government	  entities,	  value	  chain	  players	  and	  service	  providers	  are	  aligned.
