ABSTRACT
The﻿uncontrolled﻿ urbanization﻿ in﻿African﻿ cities﻿ with﻿ inadequate﻿ access﻿ to﻿urban﻿ domestic﻿ waste,﻿
housing﻿ and﻿sanitation﻿ management﻿ services(﻿ DWHSMS)﻿ generates﻿ landscapes﻿ that﻿become﻿ places﻿
of﻿the﻿spread﻿ of﻿many﻿ pathologies﻿ leading﻿ to﻿many﻿ public﻿ health﻿ problems.﻿ The﻿city﻿of﻿Yaoundé﻿
(Cameroon)﻿ is﻿not﻿an﻿exception﻿ in﻿this﻿situation﻿ which﻿ exposes﻿ an﻿urban﻿ population﻿ to﻿the﻿most﻿
recurrent﻿ diseases.﻿ The﻿situation﻿ become﻿ more﻿ complex﻿ in﻿the﻿fact﻿that,﻿ the﻿performance﻿ of﻿the﻿
policies﻿ and﻿the﻿actions﻿ undertaken﻿ implied﻿ in﻿developing﻿ countries,﻿ is﻿not﻿known﻿ in﻿a﻿precisely.﻿ The﻿
information﻿ sources ﻿are﻿dispersed, ﻿old﻿and﻿not﻿coordinated.﻿ ICT﻿can,﻿at﻿the﻿service ﻿of﻿the﻿great﻿ causes﻿
like﻿the﻿sustainable﻿ urbanization﻿ and/or﻿ environmental﻿ health,﻿ constitute﻿ the﻿irreplaceable﻿ ones﻿ and﻿
essential﻿ decision-making﻿ tools.﻿ For﻿this﻿purpose,﻿ integrated﻿ and﻿interoperable﻿ YUSIIP﻿ platform﻿ has﻿
proposed﻿ and﻿deployed.﻿ The﻿objective﻿ of﻿this﻿article﻿ is﻿to﻿present﻿ this﻿Domestic﻿ platform﻿ and﻿to﻿show﻿
its﻿contribution﻿in(﻿DWHSMS).

The﻿needs﻿ for﻿urban﻿ stakeholders﻿ in﻿the﻿processes﻿ management﻿ of﻿sustainable﻿ urbanization﻿ are﻿varied﻿

construction﻿of﻿multi-source﻿information﻿systems﻿and﻿Master﻿Data﻿Management(﻿MDM).
Integration﻿ is﻿operation﻿ which﻿ consists﻿ of﻿assembling﻿ the﻿various﻿ parts﻿ of﻿a﻿system﻿ and﻿to﻿ensure﻿
their﻿compatibility ﻿as﻿well﻿as﻿the﻿efficient ﻿operation ﻿of﻿the﻿complete ﻿system. ﻿The﻿concept ﻿of﻿integration﻿
can﻿apply ﻿to﻿various ﻿levels ﻿or﻿objects: ﻿at﻿the﻿organizational ﻿unit﻿level, ﻿processes, ﻿computer ﻿applications,﻿
software﻿ base﻿ and﻿material.﻿ On﻿the﻿computer﻿ applications﻿ level﻿ which﻿ relates﻿ to﻿the﻿problems﻿ of﻿the﻿
DWHSMS﻿ particularly(﻿ Reix,﻿ et﻿al.,﻿2016)﻿ one﻿distinguishes﻿ two﻿possible﻿ approaches﻿ to﻿the﻿problem.﻿
The﻿priory﻿ integration﻿ which﻿ is﻿matching﻿ to﻿systems﻿ thought﻿ at﻿the﻿beginning﻿ of﻿the﻿construction﻿ of﻿
a﻿single﻿ integrated﻿ information﻿ system﻿ or﻿Management﻿ Information﻿ System(﻿ MIS),﻿ recovering﻿ the﻿
whole﻿ functional﻿ fields﻿ and﻿various﻿ levels﻿ of﻿piloting.﻿ The﻿posterior﻿ integration﻿ which﻿ consists﻿ in﻿
arranging﻿ the﻿relations﻿ between﻿ conceived﻿ systems﻿ in﻿an﻿independent﻿ way,﻿ to﻿constitute﻿ a“﻿unified”﻿
system﻿or﻿meta-system.
Compared﻿to﻿a﻿posterior﻿integration,﻿the﻿recent﻿solutions﻿concern:
•﻿EAI(﻿Enterprise ﻿Application ﻿Integration) ﻿which ﻿consist ﻿of﻿replacing ﻿the﻿multiple ﻿links﻿
(footbridges) ﻿between ﻿applications ﻿by﻿the﻿construction ﻿of﻿a﻿platform ﻿centralized ﻿exchange﻿
ensuring ﻿the﻿communication ﻿and﻿the﻿exchanges ﻿between ﻿these ﻿applications ﻿by﻿diffusion ﻿messages﻿
(Avignon,﻿Joguet,﻿&﻿Pezziardi,﻿2000);

and﻿ensuring ﻿they﻿are﻿making ﻿up﻿in﻿accordance ﻿with﻿common ﻿formats. ﻿These ﻿systems ﻿are﻿
especially﻿used﻿in﻿the﻿field﻿of﻿Decision﻿Support﻿Systems;
•﻿The﻿ERP(﻿Enterprise ﻿Resource ﻿Planning) ﻿which ﻿are﻿integrated ﻿information ﻿systems ﻿composed ﻿of﻿
modules, ﻿conceived ﻿in﻿a﻿coherent ﻿way﻿and﻿sharing ﻿a﻿common ﻿database. ﻿The﻿ERP﻿are﻿standardized,﻿

units,﻿ carefully﻿ connected﻿ to﻿each﻿ other(﻿ modules).﻿ The﻿harmonization﻿ concerns﻿ not﻿only﻿ on﻿the﻿
